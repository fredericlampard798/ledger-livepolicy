Ledger: Portfolio Viewer

Effective July 4, 2026
Short version: This extension collects nothing. It has no server, no analytics, and no accounts. The only network traffic consists of read-only requests to public blockchain explorers for the addresses you choose to add.

What the extension does

Ledger: Portfolio Viewer displays the balances of public EVM wallet addresses you add, using the public Blockscout explorer APIs for Ethereum, Arbitrum, Optimism, Base, and Polygon. It is 100% read-only: it cannot sign, send, approve, or move anything.

Data stored on your device

  • Wallet addresses and labels you add — stored locally via the browser's extension storage.
  • Cached balance snapshots — so the dashboard opens instantly.
  • UI preferences (selected chain tab, whether onboarding was shown).

This data never leaves your device and can be erased at any time from Settings → Data → Erase all, or by removing the extension.

Data sent over the network

When you view a portfolio, the extension sends the wallet address to the public Blockscout explorer of each chain (the same request your browser would make if you opened the explorer website). No cookies, identifiers, or other data accompany these requests. The explorers' own privacy policies apply to their processing.

What we never collect or ask for

  • Private keys, PINs, or passwords — never.
  • Connection to your Ledger device — the extension has no such capability.
  • Analytics, telemetry, browsing history, or personal information.

Permissions

  • storage — save your wallet list and cache locally.
  • Blockscout hosts — fetch public balance data for the five chains.
